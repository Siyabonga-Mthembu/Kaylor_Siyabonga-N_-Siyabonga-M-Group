The Pharaoh was at the lead of a complicated social order in Ancient Egypt. Their artwork powerfully captures this social system, with each placement indicating its significance and position. The sovereign of gods and goddesses in Egyptian life were symbolized by their frequently life size representations. Since Pharaohs to be supernatural beings or entities in and of themselves. Their majesty and divine right to rule were also emphasized. According to Exodus the gods and kings, portrays that art was not produced for its own sake, whether it was to honor the gods, remember the deceased, or record historical occurrences, every piece had a function . To respect gods and ask for their assistance, temples were decorated with massive statues and elaborate hieroglyphics.

The decorations of commonplace items in the Ancient Egyptian, shows how art and society were interconnected. Care was taken in the creation of jewelers, pottery, and even furniture which frequently had symbolic or religious designs. This focus on beauty and details in commonplace objects reflect to a cultural that embrace art as a way of life rather than merely a verse for expressing religion.
Ancient Egyptians art also served as a medium of communication. Their writing system was widely applied in inscriptions that served as a record of the pharaohs deeds for future generations, a description of religious ceremonies and announcement of law
